# v1.3 Research Summary: Runtime Trust & Honest Controls

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Inputs:**
- `CODEBASE-CONTEXT.md` — local codebase reconnaissance and gap analysis
- `EXTERNAL-RESEARCH.md` — adjacent tooling and market-pattern research

## Key Findings

- The repo has no active milestone after shipped `v1.2 Remote Execution Foundations`.
- The highest-leverage local gaps are target persistence durability, delivery-policy enforcement, daemon lifecycle control, artifact safety, and stale docs/CLI UX.
- Adjacent tools increasingly converge on parallel agents, worktree isolation, review/rework loops, async issue-to-PR delegation, and attention dashboards.
- `tmuxicate` should differentiate as a local-first, vendor-neutral, file-backed, inspectable coordination control plane rather than racing toward opaque autonomy.
- Risk review recommends identity/permissions, daemon lifecycle, delivery policy, target dispatch durability, and worktree safety before richer remote/cloud transports.

## Resulting Milestone Choice

`v1.3 Runtime Trust & Honest Controls` was selected as a hardening milestone before worktree isolation, authenticated remote worker bootstrap, cross-run attention, or multi-coordinator topology.

## Deferred Feature Directions

1. Worktree-aware execution isolation.
2. Authenticated remote worker bootstrap and non-shell transport.
3. Cross-run attention and recovery dashboard.
4. Multi-coordinator or nested-team topology.
